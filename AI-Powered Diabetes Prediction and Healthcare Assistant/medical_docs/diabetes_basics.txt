Diabetes is a chronic medical condition that affects how the body processes blood sugar (glucose).

There are three main types of diabetes:

1. Type 1 Diabetes
2. Type 2 Diabetes
3. Gestational Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is the most common form and is often associated with obesity, poor diet, lack of exercise, and genetic factors.

High blood glucose levels over a long period can damage the heart, kidneys, eyes, nerves, and blood vessels.

Early detection and lifestyle changes can significantly reduce complications.
